Fourth Quarter School Activities

Math Month

Reflection Questions:
What is the most important thing I learned from the event?
Math Garden, Math TikTok, and Math Idol taught me that math is everywhere and can be fun and creative, not just numbers on paper.
How can I apply what I learned in real-life situations?
I can apply math in everyday life, like problem-solving, planning, budgeting, or even creating fun projects and content.
Did I actively participate in the event? How?
I actively participated in Math TikTok and supported my batch and classmates in Math Garden and Math Idol by cheering them on and encouraging their efforts.
If I were to teach this topic to a classmate, how would I explain it?
I would explain that math isn’t just in books—it’s in games, videos, challenges, and even in creative activities, and that everyone can enjoy it.
Why is it important to have an event (per subject)?
Events like Math Month make learning interactive and enjoyable, showing students how to apply subject skills creatively while promoting teamwork and participation.

Miting De Avance

Reflection Questions:
What is the most important thing I learned from the event?
As one of the candidates for Grade 10 representative, I learned to be true to myself and to understand what it takes to represent my batch. I realized that leadership involves listening, supporting others, and earning their trust.
How can I apply what I learned in real-life situations?
I can apply this by being honest and responsible in school projects, group activities, and other situations where leadership and representation are needed.
Did I actively participate in the event? How?
Yes, I actively participated by preparing my campaign, presenting my ideas, and presenting my speech for my batch to hear.
If I were to teach this topic to a classmate, how would I explain it?
I would explain that MDA (Miting de Avance) is a school-wide meeting where students meet the candidates running for school government, teaching the value of leadership, responsibility, and teamwork.
Why is it important to have an event (per subject)?
Events like MDA are important because they encourage student participation, leadership skills, and a sense of responsibility while giving everyone a chance to learn, engage actively, and vote wisely.

Street Dance Competition

Reflection Questions:
What is the most important thing I learned from the event?
Hard work and teamwork always pay off. In this competition, Fidelity was paired with Fairness. Even though our representatives were from two different sections, we enjoyed our time together and I realized that teamwork, communication, and supporting each other are key to success.
How can I apply what I learned in real-life situations?
I can apply this by collaborating effectively in group projects, activities, or any team-based situation, making sure everyone contributes and feels included.
Did I actively participate in the event? How?
I competed as one of the dancers for Team Rival, which placed 3rd, giving my best effort and supporting my teammates throughout the competition.
If I were to teach this topic to a classmate, how would I explain it?
I would explain that street dance competitions show the importance of practice, teamwork, and coordination, and that success comes from working together and giving your best.
Why is it important to have an event (per subject)?
Events like this are important because they develop discipline, collaboration, and creativity while allowing students to showcase talents and have fun learning outside the classroom.
